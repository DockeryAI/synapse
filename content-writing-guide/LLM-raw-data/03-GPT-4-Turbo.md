### 2. Content Frameworks That Convert

**AIDA (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action)**: This classic marketing model works effectively in sales copy. For instance, a landing page might capture attention with a bold headline, build interest with compelling benefits, amplify desire with testimonials, and prompt action with a strong CTA.

**Psychological Sequencing for Maximum Impact**: Positioning content to first address a pain point, followed by amplifying the problem, and concluding with a solution, can guide the audience through a psychological journey that makes the solution appear more desirable.

**Trust Ladders and Credibility Building**: Start by sharing easily verifiable facts or low-risk propositions to build trust gradually. Over time, introduce higher-commitment asks as the audience’s trust in the brand solidifies.

**Problem-Agitate-Solve Mastery**: Identify a problem, agitate it by discussing its consequences, and present your product or service as the solution. For example, a cybersecurity company might highlight the rise in data breaches (problem), discuss the impact of a breach (agitate), and then introduce their secure software solution (solve).
